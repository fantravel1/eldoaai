Anticipatory Postural Adjustments

Detailed Explanation

Anticipatory postural adjustments (APAs) are pre-programmed stabilizing actions that occur just before voluntary movement. They minimize unwanted sway, protect vulnerable joints, and let the prime movers do their job efficiently. In ELDOA, axial length and segmental decompression reduce “noise” in the system so APAs can be smaller, faster, and more precise.

Training typically emphasizes junctions that bias whole-body balance—thoracolumbar and lumbosacral—layered with diaphragm-driven breathing to tune intra-abdominal pressure. Sustained 60-second holds reinforce the proprioceptive map; over time, daily motions like reaching, stepping, and lifting feel smoother because the body “gets ready” with less strain and better alignment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are APAs the same as “bracing”?

Not exactly—APAs are subtle, automatic, and well-timed; brute-force bracing is often late and inefficient. ELDOA favors the former.

How long do results take?

Many notice smoother balance and less “grip” within 1–2 weeks of daily short holds; durability builds over months with consistent practice.

Which cues matter most?

Axial length (grow tall), soft neck/jaw, ribcage over pelvis, and quiet, low nasal breathing to tune pressure without excess tension.

Can kids or older adults train APAs?

Yes—use supported setups, prioritize comfort and breathing, and keep holds brief and playful.

Any red flags?

Unexplained pain, neurological changes, or dizziness require medical evaluation. Modify or pause if symptoms increase.
