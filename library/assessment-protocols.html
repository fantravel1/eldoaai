Assessment Protocols

Special tests and observations help determine which exercises you need. Think of it like a coach checking your strengths and weaknesses before planning practice.

ELDOA Encyclopedia

Effective ELDOA practice begins with careful evaluation. Assessment protocols look beyond isolated pain points to identify the primary dysfunctions and the compensatory strategies your body has developed. This includes movement assessment to reveal dynamic limitations that static posture cannot capture. Observers pay attention to the quality of your motion—not just the amount—while goniometric measurements provide objective data on restricted segments. Sport‑specific functional tests ensure that improvements in flexibility translate to better performance rather than merely increasing range【844102180325802†L899-L914】.

The frequency and intensity of assessments vary with your stage of intervention. Workplace integration programs may track progress monthly, while athletes often need evaluations around season transitions to adapt protocols to changing demands. During post‑injury recovery, assessments may occur nearly daily to balance progression with tissue healing. Performance optimization uses video analysis and performance metrics to identify subtle limitations that can restrict elite function. Self‑assessment skills empower you to adapt your home practice based on daily variations, making ELDOA a dynamic tool rather than a rigid routine.

This comprehensive evaluation process ensures that ELDOA recommendations are specific and adaptive. Rather than prescribing generic protocols, practitioners use assessment findings to tailor decompression exercises and sequence progressions. As your alignment and movement quality improve, your program evolves, preventing plateau and ensuring that structural changes support functional goals like sport performance or pain‑free daily activity.

Visual Guide

Use these simple assessments to explore your own movement quality and restrictions. They are not diagnostic but can inform your practice.

Full‑Body Movement Scan

Perform a deep squat with arms overhead and note where you feel tightness or compensation. This global scan highlights segments that may need decompression.

Segmental Range Assessment

Test rotation at T4–T8 or L5–S1 by sitting upright and gently rotating your trunk. Compare sides and identify asymmetry or stiffness to target with ELDOA.

Sport‑Specific Functional Test

Mimic a key movement from your sport—such as a baseball pitcher’s wind‑up or a golf swing—while maintaining spinal alignment. Observe where form breaks down to guide targeted drills.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are ELDOA assessment protocols?

They are systematic tests and observations that identify your primary dysfunctions and compensations. Unlike standard orthopedic exams, they focus on how you move and how different segments interact.

Why does movement quality matter more than range?

Range of motion alone doesn’t tell you if you can control that range. Quality encompasses timing, smoothness and segmental coordination, which determine how safely you can move and transmit forces【844102180325802†L899-L914】.

How often should assessments be done?

General wellness programs may assess monthly. Athletes benefit from assessments during season transitions, while injured individuals require more frequent checks to guide progression【844102180325802†L899-L914】.

Can I assess myself at home?

Yes. ELDOA encourages self‑assessment. Simple movement scans and awareness of alignment empower you to modify your practice as your body changes.

What tests are included?

Assessments include postural analysis, goniometry, movement quality observation and sport‑specific functional tests. They identify where you’re restricted or compensating so your program can be tailored【844102180325802†L899-L914】.

How do assessments influence my program?

Results guide which decompression drills you perform and how you progress. They ensure improvements in flexibility translate to functional performance and prevent symptom recurrence.
