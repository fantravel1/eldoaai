Detailed Explanation

Anterior pelvic tilt (APT) is a posture pattern where the front of the pelvis drops and the back rises, deepening the lumbar curve. This can increase compressive stress at the lumbosacral junction and alter hip mechanics. ELDOA programming uses targeted segmental decompression and precise alignment cues to retrain pelvic–lumbar organization and proprioception.

Practical starting points often include L5–S1 and L4–L5 to reduce excessive extension tone, followed by thoracolumbar junction work and hip decoaptation drills. One‑minute sustained holds with controlled breathing help “map” neutral, leading to durable changes in standing, walking, and sport.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is anterior pelvic tilt?

It’s when the front of the pelvis tips down and the back rises, often increasing the lower‑back curve and changing how hips and spine share load.

How does ELDOA help?

By using precise 60‑second holds to decompress key segments, refine alignment, and build the awareness needed to keep neutral in daily life.

Where should I start?

Commonly at L5–S1 and L4–L5; add thoracolumbar junction work and hip decoaptation according to screening.

How often should I practice?

Daily during correction; 3–4×/week for maintenance while layering posture checks into walking, sitting, and sport.

Is this suitable for kids or older adults?

Yes—use supports, prioritize comfort and breathing, and avoid any sharp pain or forced extension ranges.

What benefits are typical?

Neutralized pelvic tilt, decreased lumbar tightness, better hip loading and gait, plus improved body awareness for self‑management.

Any precautions?

Screen for red flags; start with supported, non‑provocative ranges; progress by response and quality rather than by timelines.
