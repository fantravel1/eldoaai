Frequently Asked Questions

What is active spinal decompression?

Active spinal decompression involves using your own body to create space between vertebrae by holding precise positions and engaging specific muscles, rather than relying on external devices.

How does ELDOA achieve spinal decompression?

ELDOA positions are designed to target particular spinal levels. By maintaining a one‚Äëminute hold while engaging fascia and deep stabilizers, you assist in balancing tension and optimize alignment.

Which positions are commonly used?

Seated overhead reach for lumbar decompression and supine cervical stretches are common examples. The position chosen depends on which segment needs relief.

How long should each position be held?

About one minute is standard. This duration allows tissues to adapt and for proprioceptive feedback to improve.

Will I feel immediate relief?

Some people notice relief quickly, while for others improvements build over time. Consistency and precise engagement are key factors.

Are there precautions?

Always avoid forcing into pain. Work within your comfort range, and consult a professional if you have serious spinal conditions.

How is this different from passive traction?

Passive traction pulls the spine apart externally. Active decompression uses your own muscular engagement, which enhances neuromuscular control and typically results in longer‚Äëlasting benefits.
