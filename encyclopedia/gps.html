Understanding Your Body's GPS

Just as a GPS device uses satellites to determine your location and guide you to your destination, your vestibular system uses specialized sensors in your inner ear to determine your position in space and guide your movements. This remarkable system operates 24/7, processing information about head position, rotation, and acceleration‚Äîall without you consciously thinking about it.

The vestibular system works in concert with proprioception (body position sense) and vision to create your complete spatial awareness. When these systems work harmoniously, movement feels effortless. When they're compromised, even simple tasks like walking or standing become challenging.
