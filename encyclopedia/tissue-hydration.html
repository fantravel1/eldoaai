Tissue Hydration

Overview

The amount of water in your body's tissues like muscles and discs. ELDOA helps pump water into your spine like watering a plant to keep it healthy and flexible.

Detailed Description

Tissue hydration refers to the water content within the body's connective tissues, including intervertebral discs, fascia, cartilage, and muscle tissue. Proper hydration is essential for tissue elasticity, nutrient transport, waste removal, and overall structural integrity.

Intervertebral discs are particularly dependent on hydration. The nucleus pulposus—the gel-like center of each disc—is approximately 80% water in healthy young adults. This water content decreases with age and prolonged compression, contributing to disc degeneration and height loss. During daily activities, discs lose fluid under compressive loads; overnight rest allows partial rehydration through osmotic imbibition.

ELDOA exercises create decompressive forces that facilitate disc rehydration by reducing intradiscal pressure and creating space for fluid to enter. The sustained holds characteristic of ELDOA practice encourage this fluid exchange, supporting disc health and spinal function. Additionally, the fascial stretching component of ELDOA promotes hydration throughout the myofascial system.

Hydration Benefits

  • Improved disc height and shock absorption
  • Enhanced nutrient delivery to avascular tissues
  • Better fascial glide and tissue mobility
  • Reduced friction between tissue layers
  • Improved viscoelastic tissue properties
  • Support for tissue repair processes

Factors Affecting Hydration

  • Prolonged sitting or standing (compression)
  • Age-related changes in tissue composition
  • Systemic hydration status
  • Movement patterns and loading history
  • Sleep quality and duration
  • Inflammatory conditions
