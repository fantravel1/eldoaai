Movement Analysis

Overview

Watching how someone moves to find problems and make them better. Like being a detective for your body's movements, analyzing patterns to optimize function and prevent injury.

Detailed Description

Movement analysis is the systematic observation and assessment of how an individual performs various movements, from basic activities like walking and bending to complex athletic maneuvers. In the ELDOA context, movement analysis helps practitioners identify compensatory patterns, mobility restrictions, and areas of dysfunction that may benefit from targeted intervention.

A comprehensive movement analysis examines multiple factors: joint range of motion, muscle activation patterns, fascial restrictions, postural alignment, and the coordination between body segments. This assessment informs the selection of appropriate ELDOA exercises and helps track progress over time.

Modern movement analysis may incorporate video analysis, force plates, and motion capture technology, though skilled practitioners can identify significant dysfunctions through careful visual observation. The goal is to understand how an individual's unique movement patterns relate to their symptoms, limitations, or performance goals.

Assessment Components

  • Static postural alignment evaluation
  • Dynamic movement pattern observation
  • Joint range of motion testing
  • Muscle length and strength assessment
  • Gait analysis and locomotion patterns
  • Sport or activity-specific movements

Common Findings

  • Asymmetrical movement patterns
  • Compensatory strategies for restrictions
  • Muscle imbalances and weaknesses
  • Fascial adhesions limiting mobility
  • Coordination deficits between segments
  • Breathing pattern dysfunctions
