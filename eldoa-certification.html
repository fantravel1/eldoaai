The ELDOA certification program comprises six levels. Level 4 is the first level permitting practitioners to teach classes professionally.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the ELDOA certification levels?
ELDOA certification has six levels. Levels 1-3 are student and advanced student levels. Level 4 (Certified ELDOA Trainer) is the first level permitting practitioners to teach classes professionally. Level 5 is Certified Therapist. Level 6 was reserved exclusively for Guy VOYER, the ELDOA creator.
How do I become a certified ELDOA trainer?
Start with Level 1-2 courses (usually 4-5 days combined), then progress to Level 3, and finally Level 4 to become a certified trainer. You must complete each level before advancing. Training is available at official centers in Dallas, Seal Beach, London, Toronto, Montreal, and NYC.
How much does ELDOA certification cost?
Costs vary by location and level. Level 1-2 courses typically range from $1,100-1,550 USD/CAD/GBP. Toronto offers Level 1-2 for $1,399-$1,550 CAD, UK Level 1-2 is £1,100. Contact training centers directly for current pricing.
What can I do with Level 4 certification?
Level 4 Certified ELDOA Trainers can teach group classes professionally, offer private sessions, and integrate ELDOA into existing practices like Pilates, personal training, or physical therapy. You can also assist in certification courses under higher-level instructors.
Are there prerequisites for certification?
While there are no strict prerequisites for Level 1, a background in movement therapy, Pilates, yoga, physical therapy, or personal training is beneficial. You must complete each level sequentially before advancing to the next.
How long does it take to become Level 4 certified?
The timeline varies based on course availability and your schedule. Each level requires dedicated training days plus practice time between levels. Most practitioners take 1-2 years to progress from Level 1 to Level 4, though this can be faster with concentrated effort.
